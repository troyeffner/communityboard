# Ports

Ports are pre-installed synapses. They connect this node to knowledge outside its own boundary. What exists beyond the family tree. Society.

## Inheritance

Ports are not independently maintained. They are inherited.

This node's ports are its grandparent's total knowledge: what the grandparent learned from society (grandparent's ports) combined with what the grandparent learned from family (grandparent's tags). That combined view becomes this node's pre-installed worldview.

  grandparent_ports/  symlink to david-trunk/ports
  grandparent_tags/   symlink to david-trunk/tags

When the grandparent adds a new port or updates a tag, every descendant sees it immediately through the symlink. No propagation step. No sync. The inheritance is live.

## Why Grandparent, Not Parent

A parent cannot evaluate its own tags from the outside. The parent is inside its own cluster. The grandparent is the only layer that can see the parent and grandchild's tags together, in relationship, and make the decision about whether growth should happen.

The grandchild receives the grandparent's full perspective as pre-installed knowledge. This is not copying. This is how the tree grows: the grandparent's learned knowledge becomes the grandchild's starting conditions.

## Relationship to Tags

Tags learn from family. Ports learn from society. Both exist at every level of the fractal. Same structure, same purpose, all the way down. The distinction is direction of learning, not location in the tree.

Ports are inherited (symlinked from grandparent). Tags are grown locally (declared by this node and its practitioners).
