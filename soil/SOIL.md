# Soil

This is the root knowledge layer of communityboard-trunk. Soil contains compressed knowledge fibers, decomposed content from fallen leaves, and the raw material that feeds the next growth cycle.

## What Lives Here

Fibers named `{RE}f{rank}r_{type}_{topic}_{YYYYMMDD}.md` representing compressed knowledge from this tree's work. Types include sparks, returns, frameworks, protocols, threads, and leaf decomposition records.

**Fallen leaves** arrive here as `{RE}f{rank}r_leaf_{topic}_{date}.md` carrying context about why the leaf fell (content retired, replaced, pulled back). This completes the external growth cycle: soil feeds growth, growth produces leaves, fallen leaves return to soil.

**Cross-tree trace fibers** are logged here when this tree sends an internal deliverable to another tree. The deliverable routes through TRUNK and lands as soil in the receiving tree. The sending tree logs a trace fiber in its own soil: `{RE}f{rank}r_trace_{topic}_{date}.md` recording what was sent, where it went, and when. This maintains lineage without creating a leaf (internal deliverables are not external output).

**Inbound cross-tree material** also arrives here. When another tree's output is routed through TRUNK to this tree, it decomposes into soil carrying the full lineage of every checkpoint it passed through in its source tree. Engineering soil is especially diverse: it draws from EE recommendations, OPS decisions, product constraints, book concepts, and narrative patterns across the forest.

## Relationship to Other Surfaces

Soil is the bottom of the tree. Everything grows up from here through the trunk, out to branches, and emerges as leaves. Ether holds knowledge that left active circulation but was never the direct growth medium. Soil is always active, always feeding.

TRUNK is the transport layer that delivers cross-tree material to this soil. Leaves only hold what is live externally. Soil holds everything that feeds growth: local knowledge, fallen leaves, and composted material from other trees.
